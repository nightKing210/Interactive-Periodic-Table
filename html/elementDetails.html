Uses

Hydrogen was discovered by Henry Cavendish in 1766. It is the lightest element in the periodic table and plays a crucial role in many chemical reactions. It was named from the Greek words "hydro" (water) and "genes" (creator), meaning "water-former," due to its ability to form water when combined with oxygen.
